The Challenge

  • In 2009 the IT Department was tasked with replacing the county’s legacy application and bringing to an end the legacy application development program.
  • Approximately 50% of the county’s LOB application portfolio were operating on the legacy platform.
  • The IT Department did not have an established program for the deployment, development or support of non-legacy applications.

County Culture

  • County departments operate as independent entities.
  • Some departments are run by elected officials (Sheriff, Clerk, DA, Assessor, Treasurer).
  • County Administrator develops policy and is a liaison between the Board and county operations.
  • There is no mandate in place supporting the used of internal services.
  • The Budget Committee and the County Auditor are my best friends.

Assumptions:

  • “Buy before build”. Build when all else fails.
  • Departments will need to learn what it means to “own” software and manage vendor relationships.
  • Departments will need to develop skillsets to support their software.
  • I.T. will provide implementation and maintenance support as necessary.
  • The cost of software will increase from what they are current paying.
  • I.T. will seek to decrease operational costs in order to offset the increase in departmental operating expenses.

Our Changing Role

  • Review service model and move focus to “core” services.
  • Flex our service model to provide support for departments wanting support from I.T.
  • Develop apps where no other alternative exists.
  • Reduce operating costs realizing cost are going up before the we gain control.

Best Laid Plans…

  • We put together a three year plan for implementation of the largest or most complex systems.
  • Various issues caused most replacements go-live in late 2012 through early 2013.
  • However, it all happened.

Current Status

  • All legacy applications replaced
  • New application development support services
  • FTE count form 23, in 2009 to 18 in 2015
  • Interfund charges from $1.97M in 2009 to $2.04M in 2015 (+3.5%)
